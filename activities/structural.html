Structural Tags

header, nav, main, footer

What goes in the header?

Things that go in the header are introductory content and/or navigation links that help direct users to other parts of your website. The header can also feature logos and search bars.

What goes in the Nav? Can the Nav go in the header? Does it have to go in the header? Does the Nav contain links to another website?

What goes in the Nav are links that help direct users around your website. It also helps to seperate the different sections your website has to offer. The Nav tag can be nested in the Header tag, placed above or even below it, depending on purposes. It is imperative that nav links do NOT lead to another website other than your own.

What goes in Main?

Things that go in Main are the dominant and primary content that make up your website. For example if your website is about dogs, the content in the Main tag should be about dogs.

What goes in the Footer?

Things that go in the footer are typically information that are supplementary or concluding. such examples of things that go in the footer are social media links, contact information, navigation links (top of page, etc.) and even logos.
